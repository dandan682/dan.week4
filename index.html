Museums

MUAC Museum

The MUAC is a 14,000 square-foot contemporary art museum on the grounds of Mexico City's largest public university. The building itself, designed by architect Teodoro Gonzalez de Leon, attracts architecture and design aficionados.

The Folk Art Museum

Located in a white Art Deco building in the historic center of Mexico City, focuses on the diversity of folk art and handicrafts in Mexico, showcasing pieces that range from pottery to textiles and everything in between.

The Tamayo Museum

It was founded by Ruffino Tamayo in 1981, when the Mexican artist donated his own works, in addition to a massive collection of international contemporary art, with the goal of showcasing 20th-century art for the public.

The Frida Kahlo Museum

Also known as the Blue House, is a historic house museum and art museum dedicated to the life and work of Mexican artist Frida Kahlo. The museum contains a collection of artwork by Frida Kahlo, Diego Rivera, and other artists along with the couple's Mexican folk art, pre-Hispanic artifacts, photographs, memorabilia, personal items, and more.

Franz Mayer Museum

It display and maintain Latin America’s largest collection of decorative arts. The collection was amassed by stockbroker and financial professional Franz Mayer, who collected fine artworks, books, furniture, ceramics, textiles and many other types of decorative items over fifty years of his life.

National Museum of Anthropology

It is the largest and most visited museum in Mexico.

Accommodation

With a selection of boutique and contemporary hotels, luxury rooms, budget backpackers, self-contained apartments and accessible and disability-friendly accommodation to choose from, it’s easy to find somewhere to stay in Mexico. Accommodation in the city is an easy walk from most major attractions and there’s plenty to do.

Mexico also has a number of great inner-city urban villages, each with its own personality and range of unique accommodation. Further afield are a number of B&Bs and farmstays in the hinterland areas, or heading towards the coast you’ll find camping areas and island resorts.
